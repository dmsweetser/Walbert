I recently decided to have you encode content with base-64. My reasoning was that Walbert can generate arbitrary SQL, and I wanted to avoid syntax errors when persisting.

However, there was a problem. Because Walbert generates arbitrary SQL, you can't base-64 encode just the contents being committed. It's a whole SQL statement.

I then had you try using detailed, granular walbert_ blocks, but then we lose the flexibility for Walbert to redefine its schema. I don't want it to be locked down if we can help it.

Get rid of all the base-64 stuff. Then we can rethink things.
